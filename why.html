Mental Health Issues Are On The Rise.

The COVID-19 pandemic has only worsened mental health world-wide. The need for creative solutions is urgent.

What if we could help our loved ones far before their mental health issues become severe.

Studies show that our phone usage patterns can predict mental health issues before they happen.

The way we use our phones says much more about us than we think. Large amounts of the data relating to how we use our phones, such as movement, key strokes, and app usage, can be used as biomarkers for mental health. 

An example of one such study was conducted on a group of schizophrenic individuals undergoing treatment. Using the statistical technique, called anomaly detection, the researchers were able to predict relapse in these individuals before it happened. 


This visualization shows an increase in anomalies (through data collected by a mobile phone) before the  hospitalization of this participant.

The sky is the limit.

Using techniques similar to those used in this study, we can catch mental health issues earlier on. Combing these concepts with advanced machine learning and artificial intelligence technologies, we can apply digital phenotyping to wide range of health issues.
